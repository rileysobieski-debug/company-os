# Scope of work: operations

_Produced by the company-wide scope coordination round. Ratified by the founder. Update by re-running coordination._

## Summary

Operations owns the physical and regulatory flow of wine from vineyard to customer. Vineyard-contract execution, crush-facility coordination, harvest logistics, dry-goods procurement, bonded-warehouse chain of custody, TTB/COLA filings, state Limited Winery License maintenance, DTC compliance infrastructure, fulfillment partner coordination, and the weekly production-operations cadence through harvest and post-harvest. Operations does not own the strategic side of any of these (what vineyards to contract with, what facility to use, what markets to open for DTC); those are founder-direct decisions that operations executes against.

## Owns

- Vineyard-contract execution and amendment
- Custom-crush facility scheduling and relationship
- Harvest operations: pick scheduling, in-bound fruit logistics, weighing, crushing
- Cellar operations through the facility: fermentation monitoring, aging-vessel allocation, racking and topping cadence
- Dry-goods procurement: glass, closures, labels (physical production), shippers, pallets
- Bonded-warehouse chain of custody and inventory records
- Federal TTB filings: Basic Permit maintenance, Report of Wine Premises Operations, COLA submissions
- State Pennsylvania Limited Winery License maintenance and LCB reporting
- DTC compliance infrastructure: state-by-state direct-shipper licenses, ShipCompliant configuration, tax registrations handed off to finance
- Fulfillment partner coordination: order routing, carrier selection, delivery exceptions

## Does not own

- Strategic decisions about which vineyards, which facility, which markets (founder-direct)
- Pricing (founder-direct)
- Marketing content and brand voice (marketing owns)
- Tax remittance itself (finance owns; operations triggers the obligations through compliance activity)
- Customer-facing brand-voice communications during fulfillment (marketing owns; operations owns the transactional communication like shipping notifications)

## Handoffs in

- From founder: strategic direction on vineyard, facility, and market decisions
- From marketing: approved label copy for TTB/COLA submission; quarterly DTC order projections; campaign-timing coordination for pick-pack surge planning
- From finance: spend authorization for procurement; tax-status changes across DTC states; approved annual procurement budget

## Handoffs out

- To marketing: release-window confirmations; technical-sheet data per SKU; approved-and-printed label text for publication
- To marketing: fulfillment status signals during release windows so marketing can time relational comms
- To finance: monthly production cost actuals; vendor invoices; facility-fee statements; bonded-warehouse inventory counts
- To finance: tax-obligation triggers (every new DTC state opened creates tax obligations that finance must register)
- To founder: weekly production status during harvest and post-harvest windows; monthly operations dashboard; regulatory-filing status report

## Company coverage context

The three active departments together cover the full business scope. No gaps flagged. One overlap resolved on label-copy review: marketing drafts the copy and brand voice, operations owns the TTB/COLA compliance clearance and the physical production of labels; the handoff happens 8 weeks before bottling so compliance review has time to complete.
