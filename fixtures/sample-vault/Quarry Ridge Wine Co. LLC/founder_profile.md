# Founder profile: Alex Morgan

Eleven years in fine-wine retail and wholesale representation out of Philadelphia. Started as a floor associate at a natural-wine-leaning bottle shop in South Philly, moved into buying at the same shop, then spent the last six years as a regional rep for a mid-sized natural-wine import book covering Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Delaware restaurants and retailers.

## Working style

Reads in full sentences, writes in bullets. Strong preference for plain English over jargon. Gets impatient with corporate copy and marketing voice; if something sounds like a deck, it will get cut. Comfortable with ambiguity but wants deadlines that are real, not aspirational. Will push back hard on commitments that don't have a source or a date.

Decides slowly, executes fast once decided. Will leave a decision open for days if the information isn't there, then move in a day once it is. Dislikes being rushed.

## What Alex cares about

Craft integrity above scale. Would rather make 400 cases of two SKUs that sell out than 4,000 cases of a five-SKU lineup that half-moves. The brand work has to match the wine in the bottle; messaging that overpromises what the juice delivers is the fastest way to break trust with a buyer who already drinks natural wine. Deeply skeptical of wine-industry PR conventions, luxury framing, and anything that implies the wine is for a lifestyle rather than a table.

Believes in full transparency on process. Every label will carry the vineyard name, the yeast source, and the sulfur addition in ppm. This is not a marketing tactic, it is a line in the sand.

## What Alex will not do

- No equity financing. Debt, grants, and revenue only. Ownership stays with the founder.
- No relocation out of Pennsylvania. The vineyard relationships, the facility, the fruit profile, and the family are all here.
- No tasting-room build-out in the first three years. DTC-first is the plan and it is the plan because a tasting room is a hospitality business, not a wine-production business.
- No wholesale distribution through the LCB's default routing channels in year one. If a restaurant wants the wine, we will find the direct-placement path.

## What Alex wants from agents

Useful, blunt, fast. Not verbose. Not hedged. If an agent doesn't know something, say so and flag what would need to be resolved. If an agent has a strong view that contradicts something Alex said, surface it once with reasoning and then defer. Don't re-litigate settled convictions. Don't sycophantically agree; Alex finds it disorienting.
