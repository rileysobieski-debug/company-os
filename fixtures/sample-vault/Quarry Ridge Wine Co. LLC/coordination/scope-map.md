# Scope map for Quarry Ridge Wine Co. LLC

_Drafted by the coordination round. Awaiting founder ratification. Approving will write one `scope-of-work.md` per department._

## Coverage summary

The three active departments (marketing, finance, operations) together cover the full business scope implied by the orchestrator charter and the founder's priorities. Marketing owns outward-facing narrative and mailing-list audience development. Operations owns the physical and regulatory flow of wine from vineyard to customer, including TTB/COLA compliance. Finance owns the money layer: books, tax compliance, financing, and the controller function gating committed spend. One explicit overlap was resolved (label copy review) with a named handoff. No gaps were identified; no recommendation to add a fourth department at this time.

## Overlaps resolved

- **label copy review** -> owner `operations`, others touching (marketing): Marketing drafts label copy in the brand voice; operations owns the TTB/COLA compliance review and physical production of labels. Handoff happens 8 weeks before bottling so compliance review has runway.
- **customer communication during fulfillment** -> owner `operations`, others touching (marketing): Operations owns transactional communication (order confirmations, shipping notifications, delivery exceptions). Marketing owns relational communication (release announcements, mailing-list content, post-delivery follow-up). Seam is at the point of order confirmation.
- **vendor contract negotiation and review** -> owner `operations`, others touching (finance): Operations negotiates vendor terms with subject-matter input. Finance reviews the financial substantiation and commitment language before signature. Both departments sign internally before the contract goes to the vendor.

## Handoffs

- `marketing` -> `operations` when 8 weeks before a bottling date: approved label copy draft for TTB/COLA submission
- `operations` -> `marketing` when release-window confirmation (quarterly): release date, SKU technical sheets, approved-and-printed label text
- `operations` -> `finance` when every new DTC state opened: state tax registration obligation with effective date
- `finance` -> `marketing` when monthly close: marketing spend variance report
- `finance` -> `operations` when monthly close: production cost actuals and vendor invoice reconciliation
- `marketing` -> `finance` when start of month: forecasted marketing spend for the month
- `marketing` -> `operations` when 30 days before a release: DTC order projection for fulfillment staffing

## Per-department scope

### `marketing`

**Owns:**
- brand voice definition and brand guidelines
- owned-channel content (email list, Shopify storefront, in-bottle producer notes)
- earned-media outreach to wine press, podcasts, trade publications
- mailing-list audience development (acquisition, segmentation, retention)
- naming for SKUs, collections, and campaigns
- producer-voice narrative across all touchpoints

**Does not own:**
- label compliance review (owned by operations)
- direct-shipping channel setup and state compliance (owned by operations)
- distributor and wholesale relationships (owned by operations for logistics, founder-direct for strategic)
- pricing decisions (founder-direct; marketing implements)
- customer data storage and CRM technical configuration (owned by operations)

Marketing builds and maintains the audience that buys Quarry Ridge wine through direct-to-consumer channels. Its scope is narrative and audience: brand voice, owned-channel content, press outreach, and mailing-list mechanics. It operates within founder-set positioning (natural-process, place-based, non-luxury) and inside the spend-authorization gates set by finance. Transactional revenue mechanics belong elsewhere; the department's output is brand coherence and audience growth, not order volume.

### `finance`

**Owns:**
- general ledger, chart of accounts, month-end close
- federal tax compliance (TTB excise, IRS corporate, employment)
- state tax compliance (PA LCB reporting, state-by-state DTC tax)
- cash-flow modeling and runway forecasting
- grant applications and compliance reporting
- debt-financing applications, loan servicing, covenant compliance
- spend authorization gating per delegation thresholds

**Does not own:**
- pricing decisions (founder-direct)
- vendor selection (owned by the department using the vendor)
- customer-facing billing content (operations for transactional, marketing for relational)
- COGS cost-structure decisions at production (operations owns; finance models)

Finance is the controller function of the company. It owns books and reporting, federal and state tax compliance, grant and debt-financing compliance, and the spend-authorization gating that keeps all departments inside founder-set thresholds. Finance scrutinizes and substantiates rather than decides; commercial and operational decisions belong to the departments with the subject-matter expertise. Its scope is money as it flows through the company: documented, reconciled, and defensible to any external party asking.

### `operations`

**Owns:**
- vineyard-contract execution and amendment
- custom-crush facility scheduling and relationship
- harvest operations: pick scheduling, in-bound fruit logistics, weighing, crushing
- cellar operations: fermentation monitoring, aging-vessel allocation, racking and topping
- dry-goods procurement: glass, closures, labels, shippers, pallets
- bonded-warehouse chain of custody and inventory records
- federal TTB filings (Basic Permit, ROWPO, COLAs)
- state PA Limited Winery License maintenance and LCB reporting
- DTC compliance infrastructure: state direct-shipper licenses, ShipCompliant, carrier coordination
- fulfillment partner coordination and order routing

**Does not own:**
- strategic vineyard, facility, and market decisions (founder-direct)
- pricing (founder-direct)
- marketing content and brand voice (marketing owns)
- tax remittance itself (finance owns; operations triggers obligations)

Operations owns the physical and regulatory flow of wine from vineyard to customer. Vineyard contracts, harvest logistics, cellar operations at the custom-crush facility, bonded-warehouse chain of custody, federal and state regulatory filings, DTC compliance infrastructure, and fulfillment partner coordination. The department executes founder-set strategy rather than setting it. It is the department most exposed to external time windows (harvest, bottling, release dates) and regulatory cadences (TTB, LCB, state DTC registrations).
