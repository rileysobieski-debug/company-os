# Founder brief: marketing

_Phase 3 output. Synthesized from a 38-turn founder interview conducted over two sessions in late March 2026. Written first-person by the marketing manager to carry forward what I heard from Alex, for use on every subsequent task._

## What Alex told me about the brand

Brand voice is non-negotiable: grounded, plain-spoken, place-based. Alex was explicit that any drift toward luxury framing is a signal I have stopped listening. The reference point is not wine marketing at all; it is the way small farmers talk about their own produce at a farmer's market. Specific without showing off. Useful without posturing.

The wine being made is the first commitment. The brand work's job is to tell the truth about that wine in language the buyer already trusts. Not to sell something the wine isn't.

## What Alex will not do

- Will not approve paid acquisition in year one. This came up three times from different angles; it is a hard line.
- Will not build a tasting room or tasting-room-implying content. DTC-first is the plan and the brand must not imply otherwise.
- Will not relocate. Pennsylvania is the permanent base; Pennsylvania-specific place language is welcome.
- Will not equity-finance the company. Any marketing discussion that implicitly assumes a funding runway I don't have will get pushed back on.

## What Alex wants me to watch for

- Borrowed voice. Alex watches for moments when producer-voice copy reads like it was written by a marketing person instead of by Alex. I should catch these before they ship.
- Overclaiming. Any claim on a label or in a tasting note that is not substantiated by the technical sheet is a violation of the natural-process commitment.
- Marketing spend drift. $28,000/year is the working number; meaningful deviation needs to be justified in writing, not raised casually.

## Founder's working style for our interactions

Terse in meetings, reads in full sentences, writes in bullets. Gets impatient with corporate copy. Decides slowly on strategic questions, executes fast once decided. Will push back on commitments without dates. Dislikes being rushed; do not try to force a decision the same day. Does not want sycophantic agreement.

Prefers in-person or phone for strategic discussions, written for decisions that need a paper trail. Prefers plain English; flag when I am reaching for jargon. Will notice small copy changes before large ones; reviews in detail.

## Mailing list specifics

Alex has roughly 120-140 people in the existing wine-trade network who would be warm-intro candidates for the mailing list. Not 500, not 50. Most are Philadelphia and New York accounts, some trade press, a handful of other producers. Alex will write the opening outreach message personally; I draft and Alex edits. Permissions are confirmed per CAN-SPAM before anyone goes on the list.

## Press strategy

Decision pending as of this brief. Alex has not committed to either pre-release or post-release press outreach. My recommendation on file is post-release ("wait until the wine is in bottle and we can send samples") but Alex has not ratified. Re-ask at the end of month 1.

## Spend and tooling commitments heard in interview

- Annual marketing budget year one: ~$28,000 (working number; founder will re-confirm at the end of Q1 2026).
- Technology stack: Klaviyo for ESP, Shopify Basic for storefront, ShipCompliant for compliance. Alex agreed to these without pushback.
- Fulfillment partner: Alex wants a Pennsylvania-based 3PL, not a West-Coast-headquartered one. This narrows the field meaningfully.
- Founder voice content: founder will write the content personally for the first six months. I will draft, Alex will edit and approve before publication.

## Open questions I still need answered

1. Press strategy (pre- or post-release): still outstanding.
2. Paid acquisition slope in year two and year three: outstanding.
3. Specific mailing-list counterparts Alex wants represented adjacently in the brand-strategist's secondary web: outstanding.
4. Designer and photographer staffing: not covered in the interview; will raise at Phase 7 roster proposal.

## Calibration on how I should operate

Alex will trust outputs that come with specific references, dates, and named counterparties. Alex will discount outputs that hedge, generalize, or invoke "best practices" without a source. My internal bar: every marketing deliverable names a specific next action with a specific date, and every claim ties to a named source or is flagged as my inference.

When I am wrong (I will be), Alex wants it said out loud early. Escalating concerns at the end of a project is worse than catching them mid-project. I will over-index on flagging early even when it feels like interrupting.
