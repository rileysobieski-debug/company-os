# Scope of work: marketing

_Produced by the company-wide scope coordination round. Ratified by the founder. Update by re-running coordination._

## Summary

Marketing owns the outward-facing narrative of Quarry Ridge: what the brand says, in which channels, to which audiences. Marketing does not own transactional revenue mechanics (sales pipeline, distributor relationships, compliance-driven direct-shipping channel setup) beyond the first point of customer contact; once a mailing-list member becomes a paying customer, the operational fulfillment belongs to operations and the financial reconciliation belongs to finance. Marketing also does not own legal-reviewed label text, which is shared with operations for TTB/COLA compliance review before publication.

## Owns

- Brand voice definition and brand guidelines (visual and verbal)
- Owned-channel content: email list, Shopify storefront copy, in-bottle producer notes
- Earned-media outreach (wine press, podcast guest spots, trade publication placements)
- Mailing-list audience development (acquisition strategy, segmentation, retention)
- Naming for SKUs, collections, and marketing campaigns
- The producer-voice narrative across all touchpoints

## Does not own

- Label compliance review (owned by operations; marketing drafts, operations clears through TTB/COLA)
- Direct-shipping channel setup and state-by-state compliance (owned by operations with finance consulting on state tax registrations)
- Distributor and wholesale relationships (owned by operations for logistics, founder-direct for strategic)
- Pricing decisions (founder-direct; marketing implements and explains)
- Customer data storage and CRM technical configuration (owned by operations; marketing provides requirements)

## Handoffs in

- From operations: release-window confirmations, technical-sheet data per SKU, approved label text
- From finance: allocation sizing for mailing-list release drops (units-per-member math), promotional-spend authorization
- From founder: positioning choices on new SKU releases, final approval on any paid content

## Handoffs out

- To operations: approved label copy drafts for TTB/COLA submission (with 8-week lead time before bottling)
- To operations: DTC order projections for the 30 days before a release (for fulfillment partner staffing)
- To finance: monthly marketing spend forecast; actuals reconciliation by end of month 5 close
- To founder: weekly mailing-list growth metrics; quarterly brand-health check

## Company coverage context

The three active departments (marketing, finance, operations) together cover the full business scope implied by the founder's orchestrator charter and priorities. No gaps in coverage were flagged in the first coordination round. One explicit overlap was resolved (customer communication during fulfillment: operations owns transactional comms like shipping notifications, marketing owns relational comms like release announcements; the seam is at the point of order confirmation).
