# Quarry Ridge Wine Co. LLC — Company Context

Quarry Ridge is a pre-revenue natural-wine company being built out of rural south-central Pennsylvania. The founder, Alex Morgan, spent eleven years in fine-wine retail and wholesale representation in Philadelphia before deciding to stop selling other producers' wines and make one. The company is being stood up on debt and grant funding only; no outside equity has been taken or is being sought.

The operating thesis is that the Pennsylvania fruit belt is underbuilt for a serious natural-process wine program. Most producers in the state lean either toward high-volume hybrids (sweet labrusca blends) or toward traditional vinifera programs targeting the tasting-room tourism economy. Quarry Ridge is betting on a third position: low-intervention winemaking with vinifera and select hybrid varieties from a small number of named sites, sold direct-to-consumer through a curated mailing list, with wholesale treated as a secondary channel pursued only into accounts that match the brand.

The company does not own a vineyard or a production facility. It operates as a négociant arrangement: fruit is sourced under three-year contracts from three existing vineyards in Lancaster and York counties. Winemaking happens at a state-licensed custom-crush facility in Lancaster County with a signed three-year agreement. This structure keeps capex low during the critical first vintages and lets the founder prove the product before taking on real estate risk.

The brand voice is grounded and place-based. Every label tells the reader exactly which vineyard the fruit came from, which yeasts were used (spontaneous in all cases), and what the sulfur addition was at bottling (always named, usually below 30 ppm). The position is not anti-technology, it is pro-transparency.

The target customer for the first vintages is the wine buyer who already drinks natural wine from Europe and has been looking for an American domestic option that is not priced as a luxury good. Price points are set to land 2026 releases in the $28 to $42 range per bottle, which is above the commodity Pennsylvania price but well below the natural-wine import ceiling.
