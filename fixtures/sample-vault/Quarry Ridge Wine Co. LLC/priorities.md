# Priorities (long form)

The structured priorities list in `config.json` is the authoritative stack-ranked source. This file explains the reasoning behind each and what would cause any of them to shift.

## 1. Secure Pennsylvania Limited Winery License and custom-crush agreement in time for the 2026 harvest window

Everything downstream depends on being licensed before fruit is contracted. The license is the slowest external dependency (4-6 months typical processing by PLCB). The custom-crush contract at the Lancaster facility is signed but contingent on the license landing by the end of August 2026. If either slips, we push the first commercial vintage to 2027 and re-plan.

## 2. Produce a first commercial vintage under the Quarry Ridge label (target: 400 cases across two SKUs)

The two-SKU target is small by design. First vintage is a proof point and a risk-bounded operational learning exercise. Success criteria: both SKUs land in bottle at ABV and pH within our declared envelope, COLAs approved, ready for release on our targeted mid-2027 window. Volume will scale only after the first vintage has moved through DTC and we have verifiable sell-through data.

## 3. Establish DTC sales infrastructure: Shopify storefront, ShipCompliant for compliance, fulfillment partner in-state

DTC is the primary commercial channel. Building this stack before release means having the storefront, email list, and compliance pipes tested and live when we have wine in bottle. Trying to stand this up concurrent with release is a mistake. We target the storefront live in beta (no wine available for purchase yet, but mailing-list signup and founder-voice content active) by early 2027.

## 4. Define the product lineup (varietals, price point, format) consistent with natural-process positioning

Lineup decisions are operational but they gate brand work and storytelling. The two-SKU target is set. Pricing is bracketed ($28 to $42 for 2026 releases). Final varietal decisions per vineyard depend on what the fruit looks like at harvest. Format is 750ml standard for both SKUs; the Pét-Nat will use crown cap for production efficiency and stylistic fit.

## What would shift these

Market-side shocks that would reorder the stack: a sudden tightening of PA DTC regulations would push regulatory work above all else. A major disruption at the custom-crush facility would push operational continuity above product work. A large grant or debt facility landing would not change priorities; capital is not a bottleneck if we hit the timing windows.
