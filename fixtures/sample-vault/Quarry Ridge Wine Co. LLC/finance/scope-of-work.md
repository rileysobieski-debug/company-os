# Scope of work: finance

_Produced by the company-wide scope coordination round. Ratified by the founder. Update by re-running coordination._

## Summary

Finance owns the money layer of Quarry Ridge. That includes books and reporting, tax compliance at federal and state levels, grant and debt-financing applications and compliance, cash-flow modeling, and the controller function that gates committed spend against the founder-approved thresholds. Finance does not own commercial decisions (what to price at, what to spend on marketing, which vendors to use); those belong to the department with the subject-matter expertise. Finance scrutinizes, substantiates, and reconciles.

## Owns

- General ledger, chart of accounts, month-end close
- Federal tax compliance: TTB excise tax, IRS corporate income tax, employment taxes
- State tax compliance: Pennsylvania LCB reporting, PA corporate tax, state-by-state DTC tax registration and remittance as markets open
- Cash-flow modeling and runway forecasting
- Grant applications and compliance reporting for any restricted funding received
- Debt-financing applications, loan servicing, covenant compliance
- Spend authorization gating per the delegation thresholds in config.json
- Financial audit coordination (none anticipated in year one; in place if triggered)

## Does not own

- Pricing decisions (founder-direct; finance models scenarios but does not set price)
- Vendor selection (owned by the department that uses the vendor; finance scrutinizes contracts for commitment risk)
- Customer-facing billing content (owned by operations for transactional, marketing for relational)
- COGS cost structure decisions at the production side (operations owns; finance models)

## Handoffs in

- From operations: monthly production cost actuals, vendor invoices, facility-fee statements, bonded-warehouse inventory counts
- From marketing: monthly marketing spend actuals, upcoming campaign budget requests
- From founder: strategic spend decisions above the gate threshold; financing strategy direction

## Handoffs out

- To operations: approved-budget signals for procurement; tax substantiation documents for regulatory filings
- To marketing: monthly marketing spend variance report; annual marketing budget baseline
- To founder: weekly cash runway, month-end financial statements and narrative, quarterly P&L and forecast revision

## Company coverage context

The three active departments together cover the full business scope. No gaps. One explicit overlap was resolved on vendor contracts: operations negotiates, finance reviews the financial substantiation and commitment language before signature. Both departments sign the same contract internally before it goes to the vendor.
