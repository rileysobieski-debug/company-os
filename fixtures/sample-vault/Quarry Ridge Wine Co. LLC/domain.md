# Domain: Natural-Process Wine Production in Pennsylvania

This document is the industry-domain reference shared across all agents. It is updated when regulatory or market conditions shift.

## Production model

Quarry Ridge is a négociant operation running out of a licensed custom-crush facility in Lancaster County. Fruit is contracted from three vineyards (Nine Shades Hill, Caldwell Hollow, Slate Run) on three-year rolling agreements. The production facility holds its own PA Limited Winery License; Quarry Ridge holds its own Limited Winery License issued separately and uses the facility under a formal alternating-proprietorship agreement.

## Regulatory surface

- **Federal.** TTB Basic Permit is required before any fruit is crushed under the Quarry Ridge name. COLAs are required per SKU. Federal excise tax is per proof-gallon with the standard small-producer credit for under 250,000 gallons annually.
- **Pennsylvania.** The Limited Winery License (LCB) caps production at 200,000 gallons annually and controls DTC shipping within-state. The LCB also controls the distribution route into restaurants: the Pennsylvania Liquor Control Board acts as the state wholesaler for most transactions; Limited Winery Licensees can sell direct to licensed retail outlets under specific carve-outs.
- **Out-of-state DTC.** A state-by-state compliance regime. Priority targets for year-one: PA (in-state), NY, NJ, VA, MD. Each requires its own direct-shipper license, fulfillment partner compliance, and tax registration.
- **COLAs.** Every label approved separately. Natural-process vocabulary ("unfiltered," "native ferment," "minimal sulfur") is not regulated by TTB but the narrative on the label must not claim anything medical or organoleptic that is not substantiated.

## Commercial channels

- **Direct-to-consumer (primary).** Shopify storefront with age-gating and state-by-state compliance through ShipCompliant. Curated mailing list drives quarterly allocations. Goal: 80% of first-vintage volume moves through DTC.
- **On-premise (secondary).** Direct placements into restaurant-tier natural-wine-friendly accounts in Philadelphia, New York, DC. Limited; relationship-driven; not scaled.
- **Off-premise (de-prioritized for year one).** Bottle shops in the same markets. Revisit in year two.

## Competitive context

Pennsylvania has roughly 250 Limited Winery licensees. The vast majority sell through their own tasting rooms as destination businesses, not through allocation lists. Natural-process producers with statewide visibility: fewer than ten. Closest philosophical peers are Va La Vineyards (Avondale, PA) and Galen Glen (Andreas, PA), both of which are larger and tasting-room-anchored. Quarry Ridge's positioning differentiator is DTC-first without a tasting room.

## Product targets for 2026 vintage

- Two SKUs, both single-vineyard.
- **Nine Shades Pét-Nat** (sparkling, méthode ancestrale, blaufränkisch + chambourcin co-ferment, ~200 cases). Suggested retail $32-36.
- **Caldwell Hollow Cabernet Franc** (still, native ferment, neutral oak, ~200 cases). Suggested retail $36-42.

## Risks, top of mind

- Harvest timing and PA weather volatility. 2025 was a wet year for the mid-Atlantic.
- Custom-crush facility is a bottleneck; we share capacity with other clients.
- DTC compliance is expensive per state. We triage aggressively.
- The wine press cycle is crowded; earned media is hard for a first-time producer.
