The model

Each department moves through these phases. Every phase ends with your sign-off before the next can start. Any phase can be skipped, and any phase can be rejected (which reruns it).

  1. Arrival: manager writes an arrival note declaring its industry-locked primary expertise and a serendipitous secondary background (agent-chosen, not founder-directed). You accept, re-roll, or probe the hire before deciding. This step is about the person showing up, not about scoping the work.
  2. Domain research: manager researches both primary and secondary.
  3. Founder interview: manager, now grounded in domain context, asks you specialty-specific questions.
  4. KB ingestion: multimodal intake pipeline (text, PDFs, images, video transcripts).
  5. Integrations: external systems (banking, POS, ESP, shipping, etc.) specific to the department.
  6. Charter: 1-page mandate, first-month priorities, NEVER list, cross-dept asks. Informed by the upcoming company-level scope coordination.
  7. Staffing: the manager proposes the department's roster of sub-agents from scratch, each with their own locked primary and serendipitous secondary. You approve row by row.

Scope of work is settled at a separate, company-level coordination round that fires once every department has completed its arrival and founder interview. Managers cross-reference each other to avoid overlap and ensure full coverage.
